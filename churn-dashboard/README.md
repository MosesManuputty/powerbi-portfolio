# 📊 Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard (2025)

## 📌 Background
This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior in 2025. The dashboard provides insights into churn rate, customer retention, and key drivers of churn to help businesses reduce customer loss and improve long-term retention strategies.

---

## 🎯 Objective
- Monitor overall churn rate and customer retention  
- Identify churn patterns across customer segments  
- Analyze churn behavior based on Length of Stay (LoS) and ARPU  
- Understand key reasons behind customer churn  
- Support data-driven retention and customer lifecycle strategies  

---

## 🛠 Tools
- Power BI  
- Excel  

---

## 📊 Key Metrics
- **Total Active Customers (Current):** 43.99M  
- **Total Active Customers (Previous):** 40.50M  
- **Total Retained Customers:** 37.42M  
- **Total Churned Customers:** 971.98K  
- **Churn Rate:** 2.40%  
- **Gross Adds:** 4.53M  
- **Net Adds:** 3.37M  

---

## 📈 Key Insights  

### 📉 1. Overall churn rate remains relatively low but significant
- Churn rate: **2.40% (~972K customers churned)**  

👉 Even a small churn rate results in a large number of lost customers due to the large customer base.

---

### ⏳ 2. New customers are at the highest risk of churn
- Customers with **0–1 month LoS have the highest churn rate (~17%)**  
- Churn decreases as customer tenure increases  

👉 Indicates onboarding phase is critical for customer retention.

---

### 💰 3. Low-value customers are more likely to churn
- **Very Low ARPU segment shows highest churn (~6%)**  
- High ARPU customers have very low churn  

👉 Revenue and retention are strongly correlated.

---

### 📅 4. Churn fluctuates significantly across months
- Churn rate peaks around **January, June, and October (~3–4%)**  
- Lowest churn observed around **April and July (~0%)**  

👉 Suggests seasonal or campaign-related effects on churn behavior.

---

### ⚠️ 5. Zero usage is the dominant churn driver
- Majority of churn is driven by **Zero Usage (~90%+ across most months)**  

👉 Inactive users are the biggest contributor to churn.

---

### 🧾 6. Expired accounts contribute secondary churn
- "Expired" reason contributes lower but noticeable share  
- Peaks observed in certain months (e.g., October & November)

👉 Indicates billing or subscription lifecycle impact.

---

## 🧠 Business Recommendations  
- Improve **onboarding experience** to reduce early churn (0–1 month users)  
- Focus retention strategies on **low ARPU customers**  
- Implement engagement programs to reduce **inactive (zero usage) users**  
- Monitor seasonal churn spikes and align campaigns accordingly  
- Enhance subscription renewal and lifecycle management systems
